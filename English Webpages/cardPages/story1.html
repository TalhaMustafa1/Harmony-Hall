Maternal and Newborn Health

A healthy start.

Being born is hard work. Your body and brain are growing faster now than any other time in life. In the first six months, babies double in weight.

UNICEF helps parents and newborns get the care they need, especially in communities that don’t have hospitals or doctors.

Pregnant mothers come to a health facility to check on their health.

UNICEF workers travel to meet with parents who can’t come to a health center.

In some underdeveloped regions of the world, up to 60% of mothers give birth outside a health center or hospital.

Midwives can provide care and help with birth when doctors and nurses aren’t available, using tools provided by UNICEF.

A baby starts to smile sometime between 6 and 8 weeks old.

UNICEF plays a part in delivering around 30 million babies per year.

Quality care and good health in the first 1,000 days will have a lifetime of benefits.
