Climate Action

Forecast: progress

“ There is no Planet B.” - Francisco Vera Manzanares, 14

While children are the least responsible for climate change, they’re the ones forced to face its impact.
Young leaders like Francisco are urging policymakers to make changes that protect children’s futures.

Children have the right to live in a clean and safe environment.

1 Billion
Today, over 1 billion children are at extremely high risk of climate dangers.

57%
In a UNICEF USA global survey of over 3,000 youth, 57% reported experiencing eco-anxiety.

66%
Thankfully, 66% of these young people identify as eco-optimists or eco-realists, advocating for action against climate change.

In Odisha, India, 50,000 youth come together for climate action.
Students took part in information sessions, raised awareness, and learned sustainable planting.

Nkosi, 20, a climate youth advocate from Zimbabwe since age 10, uses his public speaking skills to highlight the problems caused by droughts and flooding in his community.

“I stand in solidarity with countless young people who want their voices to be heard and acted upon for climate action.”
-Nkosi Nyathi, 20, Zimbabwe
