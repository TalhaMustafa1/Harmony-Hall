Education in Emergencies

When school isn’t an option.

Humanitarian emergencies, like conflict, climate disasters, and disease outbreaks, can close schools and disrupt a child’s education.

The good news is that children can still learn outside a school setting. UNICEF works to keep millions of children learning.

In 2022, UNICEF helped 18.6 million children keep learning during crisis.

When typhoon Rai hit the Philippines in 2022, more than 912,000 children were affected by the storm and needed immediate assistance.

Because many schools were damaged, temporary learning spaces and school supplies helped students continue their education together.

The best possible outcome is for students to return to schools that have been repaired and rebuilt.

Innovative methods, like e-learning or radio programming, can help reach children left out of school due to emergencies.
When school closed in Cameroon because of conflicts, UNICEF used the radio to reach 54,000 children with education programs.
