Inclusive Education

Making education possible for all learners.

Some children can’t hear. Some can’t see. Some can’t speak. Some can’t walk

What every child CAN do, is learn-and they have the right to an education.

UNICEF works worldwide to include every child in learning, and to help them reach their full potential.

In 2022, UNICEF helped over 250,000 children with disabilities access educational tools.

Technology has changed the game for inclusive learning. Digital textbooks and interactive tablets can connect students to learning methods that suit their individual needs and challenges

Alpha, 7, is deaf and mute. Before, Alpha was mostly educated outside the classroom. But with the introduction of a new specialized textbook,Alpha now attends class almost every day.

Brenda in Uganda is 7 years old and visually impaired. She gets her lessons though a reader where the pictures are translated into spoken descriptions.

Bohdan, 7, in Ukraine, is unable to speak or move on his own. He uses an eye-tracker supplied by UNICEF to speak with schoolmates and answer questions in class.

Ari, 6, in Jamaica, is deaf. He reads a digital book that tells stories using sign language, which helps him learn to read and to sign.
