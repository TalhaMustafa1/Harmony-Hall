Child friendly cities

Uplifting environments.

Your city, town or community where you live plays a big role in shaping a child’s growth.

UNICEF’s Child Friendly Cities Initiative has built a network of local governments and leaders that are securing the rights of children in their communities.

The initiative listens to youth voices in order to improve child-friendly services, reduce child poverty, and promote safe environments and spaces for play.

Since 1996, the Child Friendly Cities Initiative has formed a network of 3,000+ communities in 40+ countries.

  • Gray - No CFCI
  • Light Blue - Country with Child-Friendly Initiative
  • Yellow - Past Child-Friendly Initiative
  • Green - National Government Program Implemented

In 2023, Houston, Texas, was certified as the first child-friendly city in the United States.
Cities can only become “child-friendly” if young people join in shaping local policies and programs that meet the needs of youth.

UNICEF USA Youth Advocate and Child Friendly Cities supporter, Trisha Botcha, worked with the City of Houston to provide mental health resources to young people.

“I have learned about the importance of recognizing youth and providing them the adequate resources they need to become the future leaders of our country.” - Trisha, 18, United States
