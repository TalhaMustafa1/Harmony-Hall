Mental Health

Just as important as physical health

Young people around the globe are breaking the stigma around mental health.

It's a lesson for all: it’s okay not to be okay-and getting help can get you better.

When young people face mental health challenges, it’s important they have someone who will listen to them.
In Kenya, among other places, UNICEF works with ChilLine to provide a phone hotline for young people to talk to a trained counselor.

After Osama, 14, lost his leg in a sawmill accident, a UNICEF-supported case worker regularly visited him to help deal with the trauma.
Over time, Osama regained hope for his future, adjusted to life with his disability, and became a competitive chess player. He recently returned to school, and dreams of becoming an architect.
