Immunizations

No one likes getting sick!

The best way to deal with sickness is not to get sick in the first place. That’s where vaccines come in.

It's estimated that vaccines save around 4.4 million likes every year

Healthy children stay in school. They’re able to help with chores at home. Their families and caregivers can earn a living instead of caring for sick children.

Vaccinated children show their vaccination cards in southern Malawi

These are some diseases that you’ve probably never had, thanks to vaccines:

Polio, Tetanus, Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, Rubella, Hib, Measles, Pertussis, Pneumococcal Disease, Rotavirus, Mumps, Diphtheria.

Each year, UNICEF vaccinates almost half the world’s children under age 5.

For vaccines to help eliminate diseases, they must be given to everyone, no matter how hard they are to reach.

Working with partners like Rotary International and the Gates Foundation, UNICEF has helped reduce cases of polio by 99.9%. With determination, polio will soon become the second disease to be fully eradicated, after smallpox.

Community health worker, Lindalva, has spent 20 years making sure children in the Amazon region of Brazil get vaccinated. Sometimes, that requires taking a canoe to visit families.

In India, Dematso spends all day trekking by foot to communities he can’t reach by car. He carries vaccines in an ice box because vaccines need to stay cold to work well.

Health workers in Indonesia ride through the streets on a motorbike,announcing an immunization campaign taking place

Ghada often leaves the hospital to vaccinate children in her community. Here, she gives five-year-old Aheen a polio vaccine.
