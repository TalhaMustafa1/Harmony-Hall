A big way to help is by fighting stigma. Young people with disabilities can be agents of change by sharing their stories on how inclusion has improved their lives.

Emmanuel, 14, in the Democratic Republic of Congo, has albinism. He says, “I want to advise children with albinism not to shut themselves off because they are children like any other. They can do anything and be anyone they want.”

Margaryta, 11, just received a new wheelchair, and can now independently move again after being uprooted by war in Ukraine.

Rere, 11, can go to school after UNICEF helped her local elementary school include students with hearing impairments.

Children who are deaf or blind come together for a soccer tournament organized by UNICEF.
