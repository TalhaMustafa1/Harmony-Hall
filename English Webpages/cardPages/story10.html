Rights of Children with Disabilities

Inclusivity for all!

“If I had a magic wand, I would like to see ramps and elevators-because we are humans too, we want to be able to go places.” - Panagiotis, 11, Greece

Panagiotis speaks out for accessibility as every child’s right.

There are an estimated 240 million children with disabilities around the globe.

Children Aged 0 to 17 with Disabilities

  1. North America: 8.0 Million
  2. Europe and Central Asia: 10.8 Million
  3. Middle East and North Africa: 20.9 Million
  4. West and Central Africa: 43.1 Million
  5. Eastern and Southern Africa: 28.9 Million
  6. South Asia: 64.4 Million
  7. East Asia and The Pacific: 43.1 Million
  8. Latin America and The Caribbean: 43.1 Million

A big way to help is by fighting stigma. Young people with disabilities can be agents of change by sharing their stories on how inclusion has improved their lives.
