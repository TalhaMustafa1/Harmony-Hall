Girls Empowerment

Girls are making great strides-with more to come!

Girls can make communities and even whole countries succeed, but with gender inequality too often gets in the way of them reaching their full potential.

Removing those barriers will lift everyone, for generations to come.

UNICEF helps teen girls stay in school and finish secondary education, helping their transition to the workforce. Skill-development opportunities are also available through youth centers and other child-friendly spaces.

After Osama, 14, lost his leg in a sawmill accident, a UNICEF-supported case worker regularly visited him to help deal with the trauma.
Over time, Osama regained hope for his future, adjusted to life with his disability, and became a competitive chess player. He recently returned to school, and dreams of becoming an architect.

In Malawi, UNICEF’s K.I.N.D. Fund, founded by MSNBC’s Lawrence O’Donnell, offers scholarships for girls to attend schools that have essential supplies, like desks.

“Whenever I see a doctor, I am inspired by them. That’s why I want to work hard to be like them. I also want to be one of those people who are helping girls.” - Laikah, 16, Malaw
