Child Safety and Protection

Youth speaking up for youth

Isn’t it a great feeling when friends stick up for you?

All over the world, young people are using their voices to help other children realize their rights-including here in the U.S.

College student Radhika Sharma uses her voice to change state laws. She advocates to end child marriage, which is still legal in a majority of the U.S. states.

Our goal is for children to not only survive but also thrive mentally, physically, and emotionally. This objective can only be achieved if they are safe, well-nourished, educated, and treated with equality.” - Radhika, 19, United States

Hiris Jamal is a child right’s advocate in Mozambique.
When asked what she would do if she were president
of her country, she imagines a policy decision making
process that includes children.

“When girls do better, we all do better.” Hiris, 19, Mozambique

UNICEF convenes and amplifies youth voices to help push for a sustainable planet. Youth in Bangladesh discuss climate issues in their country, which is especially threatened by rising sea levels.
